How to play 90-ball bingo with these cards

90-ball bingo is the classic UK version of the game. Each player gets one or more tickets. A caller draws numbers from 1 to 90 one at a time, and players cross off any matching numbers on their tickets. The most common winning patterns are:

  • One Line — complete any single horizontal row on a ticket
  • Two Lines — complete any two rows on the same ticket
  • Full House — mark every number on a ticket (all 15 numbers)

Use these printable tickets together with the free online bingo caller to run a complete game without any extra equipment. The caller draws numbers automatically with a British English voice, and you and your friends just mark them off.

Tips for printing

  • Cut the cards along the printed border with scissors or a paper trimmer.
  • Players can use coins, bottle caps or simple pencils to mark called numbers.

Why this 90-ball ticket format?

The 3×9 grid with 5 numbers and 4 blanks per row is the official UK bingo ticket structure used in licensed bingo halls. Each column holds numbers from a fixed range:

  • Column 1: 1–9
  • Column 2: 10–19
  • Column 3: 20–29
  • Column 4: 30–39
  • Column 5: 40–49
  • Column 6: 50–59
  • Column 7: 60–69
  • Column 8: 70–79
  • Column 9: 80–90

Numbers in each column are arranged in ascending order from top to bottom. This is exactly how real UK bingo tickets are printed, so your home games will feel just like the real thing.

Can I print as many cards as I need?
Yes. Generate any quantity in batches. Each generation creates fresh random tickets, so no two are alike.

Will this work for American 75-ball bingo?
No — these tickets are for UK 90-ball bingo. The format (3×9 grid, numbers 1–90) is different from American 75-ball bingo, which uses a 5×5 grid with letters B-I-N-G-O.

Can numbers repeat across different cards?
Yes — each individual ticket has unique numbers, but the same number can appear on multiple tickets. That's normal: in real bingo, many players have the same numbers on their tickets.
